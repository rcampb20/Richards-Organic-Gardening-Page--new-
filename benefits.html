Benefits

Paradigm Shift

There are countless benefits from organic gardening, and some are not even fully understood at the moment. The important item that makes all these benefits possible is good quality organic soil. With good quality organic soil your food will taste better as well as this plethora of other bonuses that I will list below:

  • Taste – your food will taste better because there will be a higher density of minerals in it, which helps to stimulate your taste buds hence the increased flavor. Fruits are usually sweeter because the sugars are higher when the fruit ripen on the tree. In addition, items do not have to be harvested early to extend shelf life therefore they can stay on the plant/tree until maturity as nature intended. The food is only going from your backyard to the tables and not miles across the country.

  • Satisfaction – There is no greater joy than picking food out of your garden that you knew you grew with your own two hands. It is such a feeling of fulfillment and accomplishment, a feeling you will not get from buying your food at the store. Not to mention the excerise and vitamin D that you get from working in the yard.

  • Chemical and fertilizer use – you might say that makes absolutely no sense. Lets analyze, with good quality organic soil there is no reason to fertilize, because the beneficial insect waste in the soil will provide the nutrients for the plants to grow. As long as you provide an environment for them to stick around you are good. Pesticides are usually used because there is an imbalance in the garden and one species start to take over. If you set up an eco-system to fester the beneficial insect, then they will keep each other in check as nature intended.

  • Natural Solutions – another alternative to using pesticides and fertilizer is the use of compost tea. It is a natural solution that is made from several ingredients including compost which is infused with oxygen for several hours. This enhances the biological activity in the liquid and is used to give the plants additional nutrients. It can also be used if you are having issues with getting your insect population under control. Compost tea can also be foliar sprayed onto the plants leaves to deters insects.

  • Reduces waste – organic gardening reduces waste on multiple fronts. Since I started gardening my trash has been cut in half. All my organic waste like food scraps, are now either composted or goes in the worm bin.

  • Pollution – because of me composting everything and reintroducing it back into the system there is less methane going into the atmosphere. Methane is a byproduct that is produced from organic garbage when it goes to the landfill and is not good for the earth. Also, if more people grew some type of food there will be less demand in the supermarket, consequently less pollution from vehicles in the transportation of items to the store.

  • Balanced eco-system – due to the lack of use of pesticides and fertilizers the biological and the beneficial organisms can florish. Most people are not aware but using fertilizer can destroy your soil. It is like giving your kids steroids to grow instead of feeding them a balance diet so they can grow gradually and heathy. The micro-organisms in the soil like mycorrhizal fungi have a more symbiotic relationship with the plant and is there for long term gradual healthy growth instead of short-term spurts.

    I have never seen so many bugs in my life until I started gardening. Then I had to learn which ones are beneficial and one that are less favorable to the plants. By keeping a balance insect pullulation you use less pesticide, which not only kills the less favorable insects but also the good one including the microbes in the soil.


Useful Knowledge

Feed the soil, not the plants. Provide the soil with organic material each growing season and the insects will do the rest. In the cycle of life the insects and the micro-organisms will eat the organic matter and their excrement will become fertilizer for the plants. On the biological level fungus and baterias have a relationship with the plants roots and thrive in the decaying material.
